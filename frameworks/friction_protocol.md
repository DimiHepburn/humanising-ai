# The Friction Protocol

**An Original Pedagogical Framework for AI-Augmented Academic Learning**

*Developed by Dimitri Romanov, MSc Clinical Neuroscience, University of Roehampton*

---

## Overview

The Friction Protocol is a structured framework for engaging critically with AI-generated academic content. Rather than treating AI outputs as finished products, the Protocol positions them as **starting points for deeper learning** — introducing deliberate cognitive friction at key stages to ensure genuine understanding.

## Theoretical Foundations

The framework synthesises insights from four intellectual traditions:

| Theorist | Concept | Application in Friction Protocol |
|----------|---------|----------------------------------|
| **Michael Polanyi** | Tacit knowledge | AI cannot replicate the implicit understanding that emerges from wrestling with material |
| **Donald Schön** | Reflective practice | Each friction point is an opportunity for reflection-in-action |
| **Luciano Floridi** | Information ethics & AI epistemology | The Protocol addresses who owns knowledge when AI participates in its construction |
| **Lev Vygotsky** | Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD) | AI acts as a "more knowledgeable other" but the learner must still traverse the ZPD themselves |

## The Five Friction Points

### 1. Generation Friction
> *"What did the AI produce, and what assumptions does it encode?"*

Before accepting any AI-generated content, the learner identifies the implicit assumptions, theoretical biases, and potential gaps in the output.

### 2. Verification Friction
> *"Is this actually true?"*

Every factual claim, citation, and statistical assertion is independently verified. This is where AI hallucinations are caught and corrected.

### 3. Integration Friction
> *"How does this connect to what I already know?"*

The learner maps AI-generated content onto their existing knowledge structures, identifying contradictions, extensions, and novel connections.

### 4. Transformation Friction
> *"Can I express this in my own words, with my own argument?"*

The learner reconstructs the content using their own voice, argument structure, and analytical lens — transforming information into understanding.

### 5. Evaluation Friction
> *"What would I add, challenge, or change?"*

The learner adopts a critical evaluator stance, assessing the quality of both the AI's output and their own transformed version.

## Why "Friction"?

In a frictionless learning environment — where AI generates polished content instantly — the risk is **epistemic passivity**: the learner consumes without processing. The Friction Protocol deliberately reintroduces cognitive resistance at each stage, because **learning happens in the struggle, not in the output**.

This aligns with desirable difficulties theory (Bjork, 1994): introducing obstacles that slow down learning in the short term but enhance retention and transfer in the long term.

## Applications

- **Academic writing**: Using AI-generated literature reviews as starting points for critical evaluation
- **Research methodology**: Stress-testing AI-proposed study designs for methodological rigour
- **Clinical reasoning**: Comparing AI differential diagnoses against clinical intuition and evidence
- **Exam preparation**: Using AI-generated answers as objects of critical analysis rather than model answers
